Market Terms

Working draft · QuietLot is operated by STY Enterprises
DRAFT — not yet in force. These terms are a plain-English working draft pending review by counsel. They describe how the marketplace is designed to work; they are not yet a binding agreement. The full working draft lives in the project's MARKET-TERMS document.

The deal in three sentences

QuietLot is an invite-only B2B marketplace where verified businesses trade behind masked storefront aliases. Relationships introduced on the platform belong on the platform — with a fair, published way to take them off it. Whoever owns the buyer relationship on an order is fully responsible for that order.

1. Businesses only, verified always

Every participant is a business, verified (KYB) before trading. The platform always knows the legal identity behind every alias, even when counterparties don't. New accounts carry volume limits that grow with history.

2. Masked identity — with informed consent

You knowingly trade with masked counterparties. Behind every mask the platform guarantees: a verified business, a ratings history that can't be reset, and recourse through escrow and disputes. Attempting to unmask a counterparty — through shipping papers, payments, packaging, or data correlation — is a violation. Identity reveal is always the relationship owner's one-way choice.

3. Source protection

Relationships first made on the platform stay on the platform for 24 months after your last transaction together. Parties who want to go direct may do so by paying the published conversion fee — nobody is trapped, but circumvention is never free. Communication is filtered for contact information; a broker's supplier list and every relationship-graph detail are confidential.

4. Every order entered knowingly

Before payment, every buyer affirms: the exact unit math, condition grade and manifest, freight terms, the dispute window for that grade, and that the counterparty is masked. No acknowledgment, no order. That record is what disputes are judged against.

5. Money

Payments flow through platform escrow and release to the counterparty of record on confirmed delivery. Payouts split automatically per hop. The platform fee is disclosed on every invoice before you order. Taxes are each participant's own responsibility.

6. Accountability

The counterparty of record on each hop owns fulfillment, accuracy, and dispute resolution — regardless of who ships. Disputes follow a ladder: direct resolution, then platform arbitration on the full record. Fraud means an immediate ban, frozen escrow, and cooperation with law enforcement.

7. What can't be sold

No counterfeit, recalled, stolen, or illegal goods. Licensed categories (alcohol, tobacco, pharmaceuticals, weapons, cold-chain food) require the license at listing time.
