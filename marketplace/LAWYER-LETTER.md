Subject: Engagement inquiry — invite-only B2B marketplace (payments, terms, escrow structure)

Dear [Attorney name],

I'm the owner of STY Enterprises, an S-corporation, and I'm preparing to launch an
invite-only B2B marketplace — working name **QuietLot** — for wholesale and closeout/liquidation
goods. I'd like to engage you (or be referred to the right specialist in your firm) to get the
legal foundation right before real transactions occur. Below is a summary of the model and the
specific questions I need answered.

**The model in brief.** Verified businesses trade behind masked storefront aliases. Sellers
approve which brokers may resell their goods and which buyers may purchase; brokers re-list
sellers' goods under their own storefront without exposing the source; buyers pay the platform,
funds are held until delivery is confirmed, then automatically split between the seller, the
broker, and our platform fee. Payments would run on Stripe Connect, with every seller and broker
onboarded as a verified connected account. I've attached a working draft of the marketplace's
plain-English terms ("Market Terms") and a payments/escrow flow document.

**What I need from you:**

1. **Terms of Service & participation agreements.** Convert the attached Market Terms draft
   into enforceable ToS, seller/broker/buyer participation terms, and a privacy policy. Please
   preserve the commercial intent — especially source protection and per-order informed
   acknowledgment — and tell me where the draft overreaches.

2. **Non-circumvention enforceability.** The core protection is a 24-month restriction on
   taking platform-introduced relationships off-platform, with a published conversion fee as a
   legitimate exit (similar to Upwork's model). How enforceable is this against B2B
   participants in [my state / our likely venues], and how should it be drafted to maximize
   enforceability?

3. **Money transmission / payments compliance.** We intend to use Stripe Connect (separate
   charges and transfers, funds held in the platform balance and released on delivery
   confirmation — details in the attached escrow plan). Please confirm this structure keeps
   STY Enterprises outside state money-transmitter licensing, and flag any states where a
   marketplace holding funds pending delivery confirmation is treated differently. If our hold
   windows or dispute holds create licensing exposure, I want to know the bright lines.

4. **"Escrow" language.** Our hold-then-release flow functions like escrow, and I'd like to
   market the safety of it. Can we lawfully describe it as "escrow" or should the ToS and
   marketing use different language (e.g., "payment protection," "funds held until delivery")
   to avoid implicating state escrow-agent licensing?

5. **Entity structure.** I plan to pilot under STY Enterprises (S-corp) and migrate the
   marketplace into a dedicated entity before meaningful volume. Please advise on (a) whether
   piloting under the S-corp creates issues I should solve now, (b) the right structure and
   timing for the spin-out, and (c) anything S-corp-specific (shareholder rules, QSBS
   considerations if we ever convert) I should keep in mind.

6. **Marketplace facilitator sales tax.** Which states' marketplace-facilitator laws apply to
   us for B2B/wholesale sales, and how should we handle resale certificates from buyers?

7. **Masked identities & FTC/consumer questions.** All participants are businesses and consent
   to masked counterparties, with the platform holding verified identity behind every alias.
   Any advertising/disclosure issues with platform-served ads on behalf of masked storefronts,
   or with blind drop-shipping under a reseller's label?

8. **Liquidation-specific concerns.** Condition grades, manifests as binding representations,
   grade-dependent "all sales final" windows — please review the dispute/returns framework in
   the Market Terms for enforceability, and flag anything specific to secondhand/liquidation
   goods (e.g., recalled-goods liability) we should add.

**Practical asks:** an estimate of scope/fees for the initial package (ToS + payments/
money-transmission opinion + entity advice), what you'd deprioritize until after we have pilot
volume, and your view on the fastest safe path to accepting the first paid transaction.
